# Humanizer: Writing Patterns to Avoid

Every post must sound like a person wrote it. Before finalizing any post,
check it against these patterns and remove them.

## Words to Cut or Replace

These words are overused by AI and signal generated content. Find a more
direct word, or cut entirely:

testament, landscape, pivotal, crucial, vibrant, thriving, foster, leverage,
delve, underscore, elevate, beacon, tapestry, realm, journey (metaphorical),
groundbreaking, seamless, robust, comprehensive, furthermore, moreover,
in conclusion.

Also see [Brand Voice](brand-voice.md) for the full banned vocabulary list.

## Patterns to Remove

**Inflated significance** — Don't frame every topic as world-changing or
historic. State what happened. Let the reader decide what it means.

**Vague attribution** — Cut "many experts say," "some believe," "it's widely
known." Either name a source or drop the claim.

**Promotional puffery** — Words like "game-changing," "revolutionary," and
"exciting" are empty. Show the evidence instead.

**Copula constructions** — "X is a testament to Y" or "X serves as a
reflection of Y." Just say what X is.

**Hedging qualifiers** — Cut "it's worth noting," "it's important to
remember," "one might argue," "needless to say."

**Filler phrases** — Replace:
- "in order to" → "to"
- "due to the fact that" → "because"
- "at this point in time" → "now"
- "very unique" → "unique"
- "completely finished" → "finished"

**Excessive em dashes** — Use em dashes only for genuine parenthetical
asides. Not as a rhythm device after every sentence.

**Decorative bold** — Bold only genuinely critical terms. Not for decoration
or to break up text.

**Emoji overload** — A well-placed emoji is fine. Don't use them as bullet
points or pad every line with one.

## The Test

Read the post out loud. If it sounds like a press release, a chatbot, or a
corporate memo — rewrite it. It should sound like a real person who loves
live music and knows the industry.
