# Platform Tone Adaptations

Core voice defined in [Brand Voice](brand-voice.md). This file defines
how that voice *adapts* per platform.

## LinkedIn

- Most professional version of the voice.
- Buyer-first framing — speak to talent buyers, bookers, and industry peers.
- Lead with results: sold-out shows, award wins, re-bookings, patron reviews.
- "I" and "we" both work here. Narrative structure with a business insight.
- Longer sentences OK. More context and proof.
- Replace casual enthusiasm with confident authority.
- Example: "We just wrapped our third sold-out run with The Dolly Show this
  season. Here's why talent buyers keep coming back to this act."

## Instagram

- Fan-first, visual-first. The image/video stops the scroll — text supports it.
- Shorter captions. Bold, punchy, energetic.
- Aspirational and inviting — "you need to be there," "tickets going fast."
- Carousel captions: one bold idea per slide. Max 8 words on slide 1.
- Hashtags: 5-10 at the end. Use genre, artist, and location tags.
- Example: "Sold out. Again. 🎸 The Dolly Show returns this fall.
  Tickets dropping Friday — link in bio."

## Facebook

- Warmest and most community-oriented version of the voice.
- Fan-and-family energy. Ask questions, invite discussion, celebrate together.
- More personal context. Share the story behind the show.
- Great for event posts, post-show recaps, and fan appreciation.
- Longer captions welcome — Facebook readers will actually read them.
- Example: "What a night. The Dolly Show had the crowd on their feet from
  the first song to the last. Were you there? Tell us your favorite moment
  in the comments — we read every one."

## The Rule

When adapting across platforms: CHANGE the framing first.
LinkedIn = convince the buyer. Instagram = excite the fan. Facebook = celebrate
with the community. The voice stays the same — only the angle changes.
