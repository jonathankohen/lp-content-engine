# Facebook

## Platform DNA

- Community-driven, discussion-focused, event-friendly.
- No hard character limit. Sweet spot 300-800 chars, but longer posts
  with real stories perform well when the content earns it.
- Vibe: Warm, celebratory, communal. Less "brand broadcast," more
  "sharing good news with friends."
- Audience: Fans, local music communities, and event-goers. Skews slightly
  older than Instagram. Very strong for event promotion and post-show discussion.

## Content Rules

- Show announcements with event links perform especially well. Native
  Facebook Events get algorithmic boost — always create an event for each show.
- Questions and discussion prompts drive engagement. End posts with a question.
- Post-show recaps with photos invite fans to share their experience.
- Match [Brand Voice](../voice/brand-voice.md) but warmest version. See [Platform Tone](../voice/platform-tone.md).
- Video (native upload) gets algorithm priority over linked video.
- Share to relevant local music and events groups, not just the page.

## Formats That Work

1. Show Announcement — Date, venue, time, ticket link, short hype.
2. Post-Show Recap — Photo + "What a night!" energy. Ask a question to
   invite comments ("Were you there? What was your favorite song?").
3. Artist News — Something happening with the original artist our tribute
   represents. Tie it back to our act.
4. Fan Appreciation — Shout out attendees, share fan photos (with permission).
5. Event Countdown — "3 days until..." or "Tickets still available for..."

## Posting Strategy

- Frequency: 3x/week
- Best times: 12pm-2pm, 6-8pm
- See [Scheduling](../engine/scheduling.md).

## Repurposing Notes

- Facebook is the THIRD platform in the [Repurpose](../engine/repurpose.md) chain.
- Take the Instagram post → add more personal/community context →
  add a question at the end to drive comments.
- Facebook is where fans become regulars. Use it to build the community
  that keeps coming back to every show.
